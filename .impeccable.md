## Design Context

### Users
Primary readers are engineering hiring managers visiting from LinkedIn who need to assess level, credibility, and shipped work quickly. Secondary readers are startup founders and other developers who will look for product judgment, technical depth, and evidence of execution. The portfolio's main job is to get the owner shortlisted for interviews while leaving a strong impression of engineering taste.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel precise, technical, refined, and confident. The tone should carry quiet warmth rather than performative self-promotion: thoughtful, disciplined, and human, with INFJ-style restraint. The portfolio should signal maturity and judgment without looking corporate, loud, or needy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a light-first editorial tech magazine direction with instrument-grade detail. Favor soft paper-toned surfaces, graphite text, a restrained mineral blue accent, and small doses of oxidized teal for depth. Structure the site around typography, composition, and case-study proof rather than visual spectacle. The first screen should lead with the person through evidence: concise positioning, clear role framing, and immediate signals of shipped work. The site should explicitly avoid flashy effects, dark neon styling, startup-buzzy marketing tropes, gamer aesthetics, and template-like layouts.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with credibility in the first viewport: name, level, specialization, and proof of shipped work should be visible immediately.
2. Use editorial hierarchy over dashboard clutter: strong typography, disciplined spacing, and asymmetric composition should do most of the visual work.
3. Keep warmth controlled: reveal personality through voice, portrait use, and thoughtful details, not through loud colors or exaggerated animations.
4. Optimize for LinkedIn traffic: the page should build trust quickly for evaluators arriving from a professional profile.
5. Favor restraint over trendiness: every visual choice should feel intentional, durable, and technically literate.
